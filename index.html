    Real estate shopping is rapidly becoming a significant shopping activity. Landlords advertise their properties on social media platforms while prospective tenants search for property on social media. Prospective tenants spend hours collecting and reviewing various real estate advertising publications. The information that is on social media is diverse and unstructured, separating social media information according to a particular domain is a challenging task.

    Conducting a search process to find relevant information on social media can be a complex task. Most existing tools used to search for information are widely inadequate since they do not personalize data. Most users do not know how to map their preferences to a query that finds a match that closely meet their requirements

    I streamline social media by mining data that relates to real estate. Data about properties listed for rent from different Facebook groups for example. The collected data will then be aggregated with data collected by the application itself. The application has location aware for sharing real estate content. The proposed application will help users to find nearby houses that are available for rent or sale. Additional features such as the inclusion of property agents will be included in future versions of the application.

Achievements

Articles About Me

  • Francistown Digital City present the online DWMPC System

    The BAC Francistown Digital City (FDC) Initiative hosted the (DWMPC) directors and staff...

    Source : BAC Weekly Chronicles

    The BAC Francistown Digital City (FDC) Initiative hosted the Department of Waste Management and Pollution Control (DWMPC) directors and staff on a webinar to present the final version of the Online Waste Carriers Licensing System. The DWMPC requested a system that would allow for Waste Carriers (municipal solid, industrial, agricultural, and hazardous waste) to register and become licensed. This digitization is meant to reduce fraud and track the issuing of valid carrier licenses as well as provide a platform for data analytics for decision-making on waste management in Botswana. Present were Deputy Director of Waste Management Department, Mr. Frank Molaletsi as well as BAC Director, SCIS, Dr Galamoyo Male amongst other notable attendees.

    Well done to the team of developers; Chandida Matiha, Nshathisi Matiha, Thapelo Phakela, Millard Thuso and Kaone Daniel-Choba.

  • Stem Festival Hackathon

    Botswana’s 2020 Hackathon Virtual Prize Awards Ceremony held recently ...

    Source : Mmegi online

    Botswana’s 2020 Hackathon Virtual Prize Awards Ceremony held recently was a massive success as hundreds of participants, including awards recipients from their various schools in Botswana logged onto the Internet via the conferencing platform, Zoom, for the prize giving ceremony that featured speeches from the host institution and its key stakeholders

    The BIUST Vice Chancellor, Professor Otlogetswe Totolo, Hackathon event coordinators, as well as representatives from the parent Ministry of Tertiary Education Science and Technology (MoTE) converged virtually.

    The Hackathon prize giving ceremony was part of the 2020 National Science Week (NSW) and STEM Festival held from 6-9th October. In his keynote address, BIUST Vice Chancellor, Professor Otlogetswe Totolo hailed the event as a resounding success for both its ingenuity and coverage despite the COVID-19 constraints. Totolo also paid tribute to former President, H. E Dr. Festus Mogae who opened the science week with a presentation themed ‘Digital Innovation As a Gateway Towards Prosperity For ALL’.

  • BAC Students win the National Science Week

    The National Science and STEM festival hackathon saw more than 150 young innovators...

    Source : BAC Weekly Chronicles

    The National Science and STEM festival hackathon saw more than 150 young innovators from across the country hack their way through intense competition finals in search of solutions to counter Gender Based Violence by utilizing their ICT skills. Representing BAC was Nshathisi Matiha, Chandida Matiha, Kaone Daniel-Choba, Thapelo Phakela and Millard Thuso.

    After 48 hours of perseverance, devotion and expert mentorship from BAC Center and in response to the event theme, the team developed an intelligent based-mobile application that processes voice tones, pitches and hate keywords that are triggered by anger to identify perpetrators.

    The intelligent mobile application categorizes threats into three classes: normal, medium, and high. If the mobile application identifies hate keywords at a low pitch say 1000hz it allows parties involved a chance to resolve issues by themselves, higher pitch triggers various levels of assistance starting from the 5 trusted emergency contacts, ending with police interference respectively.
